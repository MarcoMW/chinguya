# Arm's Length Rules

**Source**: Original game by Penzo for Simply Genius

**Arm's Length** is a 1v1 abstract strategy game inspired by Gomoku and Boop. It is played on an 8x8 square grid. One player uses red stones, the other uses blue stones.

## Gameplay
The starting player for the first round is determined by the host.

In each round, players take turns placing their stones on an empty space on the board. When a stone is placed, it pushes every adjacent stone in the surrounding 3x3 one space away. A stone is only pushed if the space it is being pushed into is empty. If the space behind the stone is occupied, the stone is not pushed. If the edge of the board is behind the stone, the stone is pushed off the board and removed from play.

A player wins immediately if either of the following occurs at the end of the turn:
- They have four of their stones in a straight line (orthogonal or diagonal), or
- 10 of their opponent's stones were knocked off the board.

Special cases:
- If both players form a 4-in-a-row on the same turn, the player who made the move loses.
- If both players have 10 stones knocked off on the same turn, the player who made the move loses.
- If a player forms a 4-in-a-row and has 10 stones knocked off on the same turn, the 4-in-a-row takes priority and they win.

## Time Controls
Players have 60 seconds for each move. Players also have 5 minutes of reserve time. If a player runs out of time, they lose the game.
