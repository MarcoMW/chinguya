# Black Hole Rules

**Source**: Original design by Walter Joris

**Black Hole** is an abstract mathematical placement game.
Players have 10 tiles numbered 1 through 10. 
The board is an upside-down triangular grid with 6 rows. Row 1 has 6 circles, row 2 has 5 circles, and so on. Circles that touch each other are considered adjacent spaces. Circles in the corner have 2 neighbors, circles on the edge have 4 neighbors, and circles in the center have 6 neighbors.

## Gameplay
The starting player is determined by the host. Players alternate placing their pieces on the triangular grid, starting with 1 and ending with 10. So P1 plays their 1, P2 plays their 1, P1 plays their 2, etc. until P2 plays their 10.

When all tiles have been played, there will be one space left empty (the black hole). Each player's tiles around the black hole are summed up. The player with the higher sum loses the game. If sums are equal, individual tile values are compared, and the player with the higher value tiles around the Black Hole loses the game. If both players have an identical set of tiles around the black hole, it is a draw.

## Time Controls
Both players have 60 seconds for each move. Players also have 5 minutes of reserve time. If a player runs out of time, they lose the game.
